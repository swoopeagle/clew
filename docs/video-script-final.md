# 🎬 Clew demo video, FINAL CUT (3:00, PPH walkthrough)

Nine scenes. Each has SCREEN (what is recorded), DO (the clicks), and SAY (the
voiceover, read at a calm pace). Total VO is about 320 words, which reads in
roughly 2:20, leaving air for the visuals to breathe. Record screen and VO
separately and marry them in the edit.

**Before recording (15 min):** `reset clew`, re-onboard with
pawsforpurplehearts.org, run Find Grants once, approve two prospects (make sure
at least one deadline is in August or September), stage one war room with tasks
(one assigned, one done, one unowned), opt into the briefing, set
`CLEW_AGENT_MODEL=claude-opus-4-8` on Railway. Speed-ramp the two long waits in
the edit: the Find Grants sweep (2 to 3 min real time) and the war-room brief
(1 to 2 min). No music, or royalty-free only. Never show .env, tokens, or
dashboards.

---

## Scene 1, THE COLD OPEN (0:00 to 0:14)

SCREEN: A quiet Slack sidebar, clock showing 9:00 AM. The briefing DM arrives
unprompted. A beat later, nudges pop into two #grant channels in the sidebar.

DO: Nothing. This is the agent acting alone. (Fire `clew briefing` off-camera
to trigger it, then crop the trigger out, or film the real 9am run.)

SAY: It's 9am. Nobody asked Clew for anything. But the morning briefing just
landed, and it's pinging the team about a deadline five days out and two tasks
nobody owns.

## Scene 2, THE PROBLEM (0:14 to 0:34)

SCREEN: Simple title cards over the teal brand background, one stat at a time:
"1.8M nonprofits" then "88% under $500K/year" then "61% chase grants with 1 or
2 people" then "$200 to $600/month for the tools that help."

DO: Static cards, no product.

SAY: There are 1.8 million nonprofits in America. Most run on less than five
hundred thousand dollars a year, and the majority chase grants with a team of
one or two. The tools that find funding cost hundreds a month. The people who
most need money are priced out of finding it.

## Scene 3, THE PROMISE (0:34 to 0:44)

SCREEN: Cut to the Clew spiral logo, then the Slack workspace.

DO: Static.

SAY: This is Clew. A grant office that lives in Slack, built entirely on free
public data, with one rule: it cannot recommend a funder it can't prove exists.

## Scene 4, ONBOARDING (0:44 to 1:00)

SCREEN: The Clew DM. Type pawsforpurplehearts.org, send. Status line shows
"Reading your website." The drafted profile appears; confirm and save.

DO: Paste the URL, send, wait for the draft (short speed-ramp), reply to save.

SAY: Meet Paws for Purple Hearts. They train service dogs for wounded veterans.
Setup is one message. Paste your website, and Clew reads it and drafts the whole
profile for your approval. No forms, no manual.

## Scene 5, FIND GRANTS AND THE TRUST BEAT (1:00 to 1:30)

SCREEN: Click Find Grants. The live statuses tick through: "Searching
Grants.gov," "Checking ProPublica 990s," "Scanning USAspending awards."
Speed-ramp. Cited prospect cards land. Move the cursor to a citation link and
CLICK IT: a real ProPublica or Grants.gov record opens in the browser.

DO: One click to start, speed-ramp the sweep, then the citation click. Hold on
the government record for a full two seconds.

SAY: Ask it to find grants, and Clew sweeps federal opportunities, foundation
tax filings, and real award data. Every prospect arrives as a card with a
citation. Click one. That's a live government record. If Clew can't cite it,
Clew can't say it. That rule is enforced in code, not in a prompt.

## Scene 6, APPROVE, AND THE WAR ROOM (1:30 to 2:00)

SCREEN: Click Approve on a card. A #grant channel appears in the sidebar. Open
it: the deadline sits in the channel topic, and the pinned brief shows the
apply link, the criteria page, a contact email, and the eligibility analysis.

DO: Approve, open the new channel (speed-ramp the brief generation), scroll
the pinned brief slowly past the Apply link and the eligibility section.

SAY: Approve a grant and Clew opens a war room. The deadline is already in the
channel topic. And the brief has the part people dread: the actual application
portal, the guidelines, a contact, and an honest read on whether you're even
eligible, checked against your own profile.

## Scene 7, THE COMPETITIVE DRAFT (2:00 to 2:25)

SCREEN: Click Draft Application. Statuses show it researching the funder. Open
the channel canvas: the draft, with green ✅ USE AS-IS tags and ✏️ NEEDS tags
naming exact missing numbers.

DO: One click, speed-ramp, open canvas, slow scroll past one ✅ block and one
✏️ line.

SAY: Click Draft Application, and Clew studies who this funder already gives
to, then writes a competitive draft into a shared canvas. Green means use as
is. The pencil marks name the exact number it needs from you. It will not
invent your data.

## Scene 8, DELEGATE AND TRACK (2:25 to 2:47)

SCREEN: Click Designate Tasks. The task board appears with people pickers.
Assign one task to a teammate; the threaded ping fires. Mark another Done, it
strikes through. Then cut to the teal web board with the pipeline and task
chips, then the App Home impact panel.

DO: Assign, complete, then two quick cuts: web board, then App Home.

SAY: Then it splits up the work. Clew proposes the action items, and you hand
each one to a teammate with a click. The whole pipeline is live on a board you
can send your board of directors. And the home tab keeps the receipt: hours of
staff time returned to the mission.

## Scene 9, CLOSE (2:47 to 3:00)

SCREEN: The spiral logo draws itself in over teal. One line of text appears:
"Every funder cited. Never fabricated."

DO: Static end card, two seconds of silence at the end.

SAY: Clew. The grant agent that can't make things up. Built on Slack, for the
teams doing the most with the least.

---

## The two beats that must survive any edit

1. The unprompted 9am briefing cold open (the agent working alone).
2. The citation click landing on a real government record (the trust boundary
   made visible).

If the runtime fights you, cut from Scene 8 first (drop the web board cut and
keep the task assignment), never from Scenes 1 or 5.
